🗑️ Texte 2 – Our Rubbish and Us (extrait)

One of the striking things about most of our big towns today is that they are very dirty. This is because as a nation, we have not yet mastered how to handle our rubbish. Rubbish disposal or putting away our household refuse and other waste in a hygienic manner, is still a problem.

But where is this enormous garbage from nowadays? The quantity of dirt in our towns and cities can partially be explained by the fact that our population is growing rapidly. More people mean more waste. It can also be accounted for by the quality of goods at the disposal of the population. Our markets today are overflowing with non-durable goods produced by home or foreign industries and second-hand articles from Europe, Asia and the Americas. According to AWAKE Magazine (August 22, 2002), « Many products today are designed to be thrown away. They are either difficult to repair or simply fragile. » The same magazine quotes a Swiss weekly « Die Weltwoche » which argues that « An economic collapse would be guaranteed, if everyone were to use his/her furniture and car for life or even for twice as long as he now does. »

Of no less importance in our society today, is industrial waste. Those who live near industries are plagued by a peculiar kind of waste that is toxic waste — consisting of dangerous chemicals which are by-products of industries. For example, inhabitants of Bassa in Douala are already suffering the effects of these dangerous chemicals.

How can we get rid of dirt? There are many ways of disposing of rubbish. The oldest method is by recycling. Another way of disposing rubbish is by burying it. In some towns, people dig huge pits and throw their refuse in them. However, the inconvenience here is that they do not take the pains to cover them. The indiscriminate digging of pits can lead to other environmental problems. Plastic bags, old bits of metal, paint and even petrol are put into pits. These eventually contaminate the soil.

Culled and adapted from Integrated Skills in English by Mungeh Simon Amaya.

Section A – Grammar

10 pts
I. Complete the dialogue with suitable expressions from the brackets 5 pts · 1 pt chacun

Question 1 1 pt

What has ______ you here this morning? (bring / bringing / brought)
💡 Hint : The structure « What has _____ you » uses a present perfect. The present perfect requires the past participle of the verb.

📗 Réponse modèle

Réponse correcte
brought — participe passé du verbe irrégulier bring → brought → brought.
La structure « What has brought you here? » est un présent parfait (have/has + participe passé), utilisé ici pour demander la raison de la venue du patient. Bring (base verbale) et bringing (participe présent) sont incorrects dans ce contexte.
Mot correct (0,5 pt)
« brought » correctement identifié.
Justification (0,5 pt)
Présent parfait + participe passé expliqué.

Question 2 1 pt

The ______ part of it is that my body temperature has risen to 40 degrees Celsius. (bad / worst / worse)
💡 Hint : Bad → worse → worst. Worst est le superlatif, worse est le comparatif. Ici, le patient désigne la dimension la plus grave de sa situation parmi toutes.

📗 Réponse modèle

Réponse correcte
worst — superlatif de bad.
Le patient évoque l'aspect le plus grave de sa situation parmi plusieurs symptômes décrits. Le superlatif worst s'impose : « The worst part of it » = « le pire de tout ça ». Worse (comparatif) nécessiterait une comparaison explicite avec un autre élément ; bad est simplement l'adjectif de base.

Question 3 1 pt

______ have you done about the temperature? (how / which / what)
💡 Hint : What interroge sur des actions ou des choses. How interroge sur la manière. Which implique un choix entre options. Le docteur veut savoir ce que le patient a fait.

📗 Réponse modèle

Réponse correcte
What — pronom interrogatif portant sur une action.
« What have you done about the temperature? » = « Qu'avez-vous fait concernant la fièvre ? » Le médecin demande quelles mesures ont été prises, non la manière (how) ni un choix entre options précises (which).

Question 4 1 pt

I took malaria tablets that I bought from a neighbour ______ medicine store is at the main road junction in our quarter. (who / who's / whose)
💡 Hint : Whose est le pronom relatif possessif (= dont le / de qui le). Who = sujet (personne). Who's = contraction de who is. Ici, le mot manquant introduit le possesseur du magasin.

📗 Réponse modèle

Réponse correcte
whose — pronom relatif possessif.
« …a neighbour whose medicine store is at the main road junction » = « un voisin dont la pharmacie se trouve au carrefour ». Whose indique que le magasin appartient au voisin. Who serait sujet, who's signifie who is, tous deux incorrects ici.

Question 5 1 pt

You are aware of that ______ you? (aren't / are / isn't)
💡 Hint : La question-tag (tag question) doit être à la forme contraire de la phrase principale. La phrase principale est affirmative (you are aware), donc la tag doit être négative. Le sujet est you.

📗 Réponse modèle

Réponse correcte
aren't — forme négative contractée de are pour la tag question.
Règle : affirmative + tag négative → « You are aware of that, aren't you? ». Are est positif (incorrect pour une tag après phrase affirmative). Isn't ne correspond pas au sujet you.

Question 1 1 pt

Most parents don't like to borrow money from the bank to finance their children's studies. They borrow from their meeting groups.
Most parents prefer…
💡 Hint : Structure de préférence : prefer + V-ing + to + V-ing. Comparez les deux actions (emprunter à la banque vs emprunter aux tontines).

📗 Réponse modèle

Most parents prefer borrowing from their meeting groups to borrowing money from the bank to finance their children's studies.
Structure prefer + V-ing (0,5 pt)
Gérondif correctement utilisé après prefer.
Sens conservé (0,5 pt)
Les deux actions comparées et le sens original maintenu.

Question 2 1 pt

"Do I need a National Identity Card in order to apply for this job?" The applicant asked me.
The applicant asked me…
💡 Hint : Discours indirect pour une question fermée (oui/non) : asked + whether/if + sujet + verbe (décalage temporel). Attention au changement de pronom I → he/she.

📗 Réponse modèle

The applicant asked me whether/if he/she needed a National Identity Card in order to apply for the job.
Whether/if (0,25 pt)
Introducteur de question indirecte fermée.
Pronom he/she (0,25 pt)
Changement de personne I → he/she.
Temps (0,25 pt)
Do need → needed (concordance des temps).
Sens (0,25 pt)
Sens global de la question préservé.

Question 3 1 pt

It is risky to have a pin code that is not secured. Hackers may hack your account and do away with your money.
Having…
💡 Hint : Transformez le sujet it en gérondif sujet : Having + nom/adjectif = is risky + because/as + conséquence.

📗 Réponse modèle

Having a pin code that is not secured is risky as hackers may hack your account and do away with your money.
Gérondif sujet (0,5 pt)
Having utilisé comme sujet de la phrase.
Fusion cohérente (0,5 pt)
Les deux phrases originales combinées logiquement.

Question 4 1 pt

My brother did not know that online shopping was cheap and less stressful so they bought all their goods from the supermarket.
If my brother had…
💡 Hint : Conditionnel passé (3ème type) : If + had + participe passé, would/could + have + participe passé. Exprime une situation hypothétique dans le passé.

📗 Réponse modèle

If my brother had known that online shopping was cheap and less stressful, they would not have bought all their goods from the supermarket.
If + had known (0,5 pt)
Proposition conditionnelle au plus-que-parfait.
would not have bought (0,5 pt)
Conditionnel passé dans la principale.

Question 5 1 pt

Cigarette smoking can cause cancer of the lungs.
Cancer of the lungs…
💡 Hint : Transformez à la voix passive. Le complément d'agent (cigarette smoking) devient introduit par by. Structure : sujet + can be + participe passé + by + agent.

📗 Réponse modèle

Cancer of the lungs can be caused by cigarette smoking.
Can be caused (0,5 pt)
Modalité passive correcte : can + be + participe passé.
By cigarette smoking (0,5 pt)
Complément d'agent introduit correctement par by.

Question 1 1 pt

Tom has been called up for many ______ interviews but he has never been given the opportunity to work anywhere.
💡 Hint : Le mot manquant qualifie le type d'interviews. Si Tom cherche du travail sans succès, il passe des entretiens d'emploi. Le mot manquant est un adjectif ou nom qualifiant ces interviews.

📗 Réponse modèle

Réponse correcte
job (ou unsuccessful) — « job interviews » = entretiens d'embauche.
Tom a passé de nombreux entretiens d'emploi sans jamais être retenu. Le mot job qualifie la nature des interviews : ce sont des entretiens professionnels. Une autre réponse acceptable : many unsuccessful interviews.

Question 2 1 pt

Eto'o is a retired footballer of the Indomitable Lions, the national team of Cameroon. He contested for the presidency of FECAFOOT and won with an overwhelming ______.
💡 Hint : Après « overwhelming » (écrasant), le mot attendu désigne le résultat d'une élection — ce que l'on obtient en proportion de voix lors d'un vote.

📗 Réponse modèle

majority — « an overwhelming majority » = une majorité écrasante.
En contexte électoral, on parle de remporter une majority (majorité) de voix. Overwhelming est un amplificateur signifiant que la victoire a été très large.

Question 3 1 pt

In most of the rural areas in Cameroon, women constitute the backbone of the ______ of their areas because they sell the crops that come from their farms and take care of their families.
💡 Hint : Les femmes vendent les récoltes et nourrissent les familles → elles soutiennent l'activité productive locale. Elles sont la colonne vertébrale de l'économie locale.

📗 Réponse modèle

economy — « the backbone of the economy » = la colonne vertébrale de l'économie.
Les femmes rurales assurent la production et la distribution des biens alimentaires, ce qui constitue le moteur de l'économie locale. Economy désigne ici l'ensemble des activités productives et commerciales d'une communauté.

Question 4 1 pt

It is important to take the Covid-19 ______ for better protection against it.
💡 Hint : Le mot manquant désigne la substance médicale administrée pour protéger contre une maladie — notamment lors d'une campagne de vaccination mondiale.

📗 Réponse modèle

vaccine — « take the Covid-19 vaccine » = se faire vacciner contre la Covid-19.
Un vaccine est une préparation biologique qui confère une immunité contre une maladie infectieuse. C'est le terme médical précis dans ce contexte.

Question 5 1 pt

Kate Jones got into depression following the ______ of her parents in a ghastly motor accident.
💡 Hint : Un accident de voiture « ghastly » (horrible) dont Kate est déprimée → ses parents n'ont pas survécu. Le mot désigne leur disparition.

📗 Réponse modèle

death (ou loss) — « following the death of her parents » = à la suite du décès de ses parents.
Kate est déprimée parce que ses parents sont morts dans l'accident. Death est le terme le plus précis ; loss (perte) est également acceptable.

⚠️ Il y a plus de mots que de blancs. Certains mots ne seront pas utilisés.

In many modern banks nowadays, transactions have become easier than before. Customers do not need to move to their main branch to [1] money from their bank accounts. They can use ATM machines around them. This [2] time. Sometimes we may be admitted in the [3] and need money. Some other person may help using our ATM cards. Many banks encourage the use of ICT [4] for many other transactions such as the payment of electricity and water [5].

Blanc [1] 1,25 pt

Customers do not need to move to their main branch to [1] money from their bank accounts.

📗 Réponse modèle

withdraw — retirer de l'argent d'un compte bancaire. Withdraw money from a bank account est l'expression consacrée. Deposit = déposer (contraire) ; save = épargner.

Blanc [2] 1,25 pt

This [2] time.

📗 Réponse modèle

saves — 3ème personne du singulier, sujet this (= utiliser les DAB). « This saves time » = cela fait gagner du temps. Save (infinitif) serait incorrect grammaticalement avec le sujet this.

Blanc [3] 1,25 pt

Sometimes we may be admitted in the [3] and need money.

📗 Réponse modèle

hospital — on est admitted in the hospital (hospitalisé). Ce contexte justifie le besoin urgent d'argent via un tiers utilisant la carte bancaire.

Blanc [4] 1,25 pt

Many banks encourage the use of ICT [4] for many other transactions.

📗 Réponse modèle

tools — « ICT tools » = outils des technologies de l'information et de la communication. Les banques encouragent l'utilisation de ces outils numériques pour diverses transactions.

Blanc [5] 1,25 pt

…such as the payment of electricity and water [5].

📗 Réponse modèle

bills — « electricity and water bills » = factures d'électricité et d'eau. Bills désigne les factures de services publics. Mots non utilisés : deposit, save.

Question 4 2 pts

What is the position of the Swiss weekly newspaper « Die Weltwoche » for a sustainable economy?
💡 Hint : Le journal suisse formule une condition hypothétique qui conduirait à un effondrement économique. Quelle est leur position sur la durabilité des biens de consommation ?

📗 Réponse modèle

Die Weltwoche argues that using durable goods — such as furniture and cars — for their entire lifespan, or even twice as long as people currently do, would guarantee an economic collapse. In other words, the newspaper implies that a sustainable economy based on long-lasting products is paradoxically incompatible with economic growth, since the current economy depends on rapid consumption and replacement of goods.
Position clairement exposée (1 pt)
L'argument économique du journal reformulé.
Implication comprise (1 pt)
Le paradoxe durabilité vs croissance économique expliqué.

Topic 1 – Narrative Essay 10 pts

Last week your family organized the traditional wedding of your older sister. You were actively involved in the ceremony. You want to let your friends get a feel of what happened. Narrate what happened to them by considering: When and where it took place · The different stages of the ceremony · Who attended and their impressions · Your general impression.
💡 Plan suggéré : Introduction (date, lieu) → Déroulement (dot, discours, fête) → Participants et réactions → Conclusion personnelle.

📗 Exemple de production

Last Saturday, my family organized the traditional wedding of my older sister, Amina, at our family compound in Bafoussam. The ceremony began at nine o'clock in the morning and lasted until midnight.

The event unfolded in three main stages. First, the dowry presentation took place, during which my brother-in-law's family offered gifts and money to our parents. Then came the official declaration, where both families sealed the union with palm wine and prayers from our village elders. Finally, a grand feast was organized, filled with traditional dances, music, and plenty of food.

Relatives came from Douala, Yaoundé, and even from France. The atmosphere was joyful and emotional. Our grandmother cried tears of happiness, while the young guests danced enthusiastically to bikutsi rhythms. My personal role was to welcome guests and coordinate the food service, which I found exhausting but deeply rewarding.

Overall, I was profoundly moved by this ceremony. It reminded me of the richness of our cultural heritage and the importance of family bonds. I will cherish this memory forever.
Contenu / Pertinence (4 pts)
Tous les points traités : date, lieu, étapes, participants, impression.
Langue / Grammaire (3 pts)
Temps narratifs corrects (prétérit), vocabulaire riche.
Structure / Cohérence (2 pts)
Introduction, développement, conclusion ; connecteurs logiques.
Longueur (1 pt)
Entre 200 et 250 mots respectés.

Topic 2 – Formal Letter 10 pts

When the Probatoire results were published last August, your best friend failed and has been avoiding you because you passed. You are Bima Rasta and your friend is Ngong Jones. Write a letter to advise him: Not to be demoralized · To join a study group with classmates · To stay away from abusive telephone usage · To choose the subjects he is good at.
💡 Format : En-tête (nom, adresse, date) → Formule d'appel → Corps (4 conseils développés) → Formule de politesse → Signature.

📗 Exemple de production

Bima Rasta
P.O. Box 123, Yaoundé
3rd June 2025

Dear Ngong Jones,

I was deeply saddened to hear about your Probatoire results, but I want you to know that this is not the end of your journey. Failure is just a stepping stone to success, and I believe you can still make it with determination and focus. Do not let this setback demoralize you — many successful people have faced similar challenges and triumphed.

I strongly advise you to join a study group with our classmates. Studying together will help you understand difficult topics better and keep you motivated. You can share ideas and learn from others' strengths. Additionally, please stay away from abusive telephone usage. Excessive scrolling and gaming consume precious study time. Instead, dedicate those hours to revising past papers.

Most importantly, choose the subjects you are genuinely good at. Focusing on your strengths will boost your confidence and improve your overall performance. Create a timetable and stick to it.

Stay positive, Ngong. You have the potential to succeed, and I am rooting for you!

Yours sincerely,
Bima Rasta

Les 4 conseils développés et pertinents.

Topic 3 – Article de presse 10 pts

You are the Senior Prefect of your school and you have realised that violence is on the rise. Write an article to be published in the « Horizon Newspaper » titled « Violence, the cankerworm in our schools today. »
In your article: Give some causes of violence in school · Identify some consequences of violence · Suggest possible solutions.
💡 Structure article : Titre en gras → Chapeau d'introduction → Causes (§1) → Conséquences (§2) → Solutions (§3) → Conclusion/Appel.

📗 Exemple de production

VIOLENCE, THE CANKERWORM IN OUR SCHOOLS TODAY
By the Senior Prefect — Horizon Newspaper

Violence has become an alarming reality in our school environment. As Senior Prefect, I feel it is my duty to address this growing menace that is eating into the very fabric of our educational system.

Several factors contribute to school violence. First, peer pressure and gang culture push vulnerable students to adopt aggressive behaviours. Second, broken homes deprive children of emotional stability, making them more prone to violence. Finally, the unchecked use of social media exposes students to violent content that they imitate.

The consequences are devastating. Victims suffer physical injuries, psychological trauma, and academic decline. The school environment becomes hostile, discouraging attendance and learning. In extreme cases, violence leads to expulsions or even death.

To combat this scourge, I propose three solutions: regular counselling sessions to help students manage emotions; the creation of student mediation committees to resolve conflicts peacefully; and strict disciplinary policies coupled with parental involvement.

Violence has no place in our schools. Let us act now before it is too late.
Contenu (4 pts)
Causes, conséquences et solutions clairement articulées.
Format article (1 pt)
Titre, chapeau, structure paragraphique.
Langue (3 pts)
Vocabulaire journalistique, grammaire, registre soutenu.
Cohérence + longueur (2 pts)
Connecteurs logiques + 200-250 mots respectés.
